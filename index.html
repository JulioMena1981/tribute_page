Albert Einstein

A genius of science

Here's a time line of Einstein's life:

  • 1879 Born in Ulm.
  • 1896 He began his higher studies at the Federal Institute of Technology in Zurich.
  • 1901 Acquire Swiss nationality.
  • 1902 He joined the Federal Patent Office in Bern as an official.
  • 1903 He marries Mileva Maric, with whom he will have two children.
  • 1905 He publishes his first articles in which he addresses the fields of Brownian motion, the photoelectric effect, and special relativity.
  • 1909 Get your first tenure as a full professor at the University of Zurich.
  • 1913 He is appointed director of the Kaiser Wilhelm Institute for Physics in Berlin.
  • 1916 He publishes the General Theory of Relativity.
  • 1919 Divorced from Mileva, he married Elsa, a cousin of hers.
  • 1920 Meet Leo Szilard, with whom you will develop inventions such as a refrigerator and an electromagnetic pump with no moving parts.
  • 1921 He receives the Nobel Prize in physics.
  • 1933 He left Germany and lived in exile in France, Belgium, the United Kingdom and the United States, the latter country where he was enthusiastically received. There he will be Professor of Theoretical Physics at the Princeton Institute for Higher Studies.
  • 1939 Letter to Roosevelt asking him to undertake a research program on the atomic bomb.
  • 1940 Adopt American citizenship.
  • 1945 He retires from teaching to be able to exclusively develop his theoretical research.
  • 1955 He died in Princeton (USA).
"Einstein's effort immediately placed him among the most eminent of European physicists, but public recognition of the true scope of his theories was slow in coming; the Nobel Prize in Physics, he received it in 1921."
